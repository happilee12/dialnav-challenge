The 1st DialNav Challenge

Hosted at the Embodied Agent and Dialog (EAD) Workshop, ECCV 2026.

Overview

The DialNav Challenge benchmarks embodied agents that follow high-level, intentionally ambiguous natural-language instructions in indoor environments. Successful agents must combine vision-and-language navigation with proactive dialog: detecting uncertainty, asking clarifying questions, and updating their beliefs as the conversation progresses.

The challenge contributes a benchmark, a starter kit, and an evaluation server. Top-performing teams will be invited to present at the EAD Workshop at ECCV 2026.

Features

  • Photorealistic indoor scenes (TBD simulator).
  • High-level, ambiguous instructions requiring dialog to resolve.
  • Human-annotated reference dialogs with clarifying questions.
  • Train / Val-Seen / Val-Unseen / Test-Unseen splits.
  • Action space combining navigation and ask-question actions.
  • Dialog-aware metrics: Success Rate, SPL, question count, dialog quality.

Guidelines

  1. Register a team on EvalAI (link will be provided at launch).
  2. Download the starter code, dataset, and simulator from the challenge GitHub repository.
  3. Train your agent and produce predictions on the validation and test splits.
  4. Submit predictions through EvalAI before the deadline. A short technical report is required for the leaderboard.

Phases

Dev phase. Submissions are evaluated on the Val-Unseen split. Unlimited submissions; leaderboard is public.

Test phase. Submissions are evaluated on the Test-Unseen split. Limited number of submissions per team; final leaderboard determines the winners.

Reserve phase. A held-out subset is used to verify reproducibility for top-ranked teams.
